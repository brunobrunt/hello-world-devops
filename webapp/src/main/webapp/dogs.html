Alabai Dog

origin

The Alabai, or Central Asian Shepherd, originates from Central Asia, tracing its roots back millennia. Bred by nomadic tribes, it safeguarded livestock and property in harsh terrains. Revered for strength and loyalty, the Alabai was a crucial protector against threats.

Price

$1000-3000

Lifespan

10-12 years However, with proper care, a healthy lifestyle, and regular veterinary check-ups, some Alabais have been known to live longer.

Size

Males: Approximately 27 to 32 inches (69 to 81 cm) in height at the shoulder. Females: Approximately 24 to 28 inches (61 to 71 cm) in height at the shoulder.

Turkish Kangal

Origin

The Turkish Kangal dog originates from Turkey, specifically the Sivas province. Bred for centuries by shepherds, they are known for their exceptional guarding instincts and protective nature. Revered for their ability to defend livestock against predators, Kangal dogs have become a symbol of loyalty and strength.

Price

$900-2500

Lifespan

12-15 years But with proper care, a healthy lifestyle, and regular veterinary check-ups, some Kangal dogs have been known to live even longer.

Size

Males: Approximately 30 to 32 inches (76 to 81 cm) in height at the shoulder. Females: Approximately 28 to 30 inches (71 to 76 cm) in height at the shoulder.

Bully Kutta

Origin

The Bully Kutta dog, also known as the Indian Mastiff, has its origin in the Indian subcontinent. Bred for their strength and courage, these dogs were historically used for hunting, guarding, and fighting purposes. With a history dating back centuries, they have earned a reputation as loyal and protective companions.

Price

$800-1200

Lifespan

8-12 years

Size

Males: Approximately 30 to 44 inches (76 to 112 cm) in height at the shoulder. Females: Approximately 28 to 36 inches (71 to 91 cm) in height at the shoulder.

Boerboel

Origin

The Boerboel, originating in South Africa, was bred by settlers as a dependable farm guardian. These dogs, resulting from crossbreeding, embody strength and loyalty. They were essential protectors of livestock and families. Today, their rich heritage and protective nature make them cherished worldwide.

Price

$1500-2000

Lifespan

10-12 years

Size

Approximately 24 to 28 inches (61 to 71 cm) in height at the shoulder. Females: Approximately 22 to 25 inches (56 to 64 cm) in height at the shoulder.

Pitbull Terrier

Origin

The Pitbull Terrier originated in 19th-century England, initially bred for bull-baiting and later adapted to roles on farms. They gained popularity in the United States as cherished family pets, excelling in various tasks including herding and therapy work.

Price

$1500-4500

Lifespan

8-15 years

Size

Males: 18–21 inches (45–53 cm) in height and around 35–60 pounds (15–27 kg) in weight Females: 17–20 inches (43–50 cm) in height and 30–50 pounds (13–22 kg) in weight.

Caucasian-shepherd

Origin

The Caucasian Shepherd Dog or Caucasian Ovcharka is a large livestock guardian dog native to the countries of the Caucasus region, notably Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, Russian oblast of Rostov, Russian Krais of Krasnodar & Stavropol.

Doberman pinscher

Origin

Karl Friedrich Louis Dobermann, a German tax collector, created the Doberman Pinscher breed in 1870. It is believed the breed originated from the mix of cattle and shepherd dogs. In 1895, the dog was mixed with Manchester terrier and at the beginning of the 20th century, Greyhound bloodline was introduced.

Price

$500-2250

Lifespan

10-13 years

Size

Males are about 27 or 28 inches tall and weigh about 70 pounds (32 kilograms), while females are an inch or two shorter and weigh about 60 to 65 pounds (27 to 29 kilograms).

Great Dane

Origin

The Dane is German in origin, not Danish. The breed is thought to have been around for more than 400 years. Great Danes descend from mastiff-like dogs that were bred by German nobility to protect country estates and hunt wild boar.

Price

$500-3000

Lifespan

8-10

Size

Male- 32 inches tall and weigh anywhere from about 100 to 120 pounds. females- 30 inches tall and weigh about 100 to 120 pounds.

Cane-corso

Origin

Although the Cane Corso's lineage traces back to ancient Rome, it was recognized by the AKC and FCI only in the 21st century; the dog was little known outside of Italy prior to the 1980s.

Bull Terrier

Origin

Bull terriers were developed in England during the 19th century. Around 1835, a cross between the old English terrier and the bulldog produced the Bull Terrier. Later crosses to the Spanish Pointer; even later, to the white English terrier and Dalmatian, produced a stylish, tough, white dog.
